Tepian Negeri Description

Generally, regions in the East of Indonesia are under-developed in contrast other regions in the country in almost every field; development, economy, social, education, health along with other public services.

East Indonesia consists of several islands such as Sulawesi, Nusa Tenggara, Maluku, and Papua. It is inhabited by 36 million lives, or around 14 % of the whole population in Indonesia. Unfortunately, 20 % or 28 million lives people in this region are still living in poverty. are designated from this region.

SCHOOL

Having a decent school to learn in, has been a long dream of our fellow Indonesian children living in areas such as Papua, West Nusa Tenggara, East Nusa Tenggara Sumatra, Sulawesi, etc.

With ceilings leaking when it rains, natural dirty ground flooring, walls, and roof made out of bamboos are a reality they have to face within the process of their education. They are also without other facilities such as libraries, sporting facility, toilets, etc.

Join now and make a warm-hearting heartwarming gesture in making their dreams a reality by sending in your donations in “Renovation of School Program” and “School Development Program.”

Waterwell Waqf

El-Nino has caused drought in most of the area in Indonesia, mainly regions in the east. The impact of the disaster has worsened the condition in areas with limited water infrastructure and state or personal sanitation public water facilities. Most of the residents are still fulfilling their daily need of water from rivers, rain water or other water resources which dry up in the summer. The situation has also affected animals and plants.

In creating a solution, ACT has initiated waqf of water program, amongst them are waqf of shallow and deep wells. Dedicated to areas in the east and other locations across Indonesia. This program will be built along with water storage facilities, pipeline installation and sanitation facilities.

As it is narrated by Abu Ahmad at a moment in time, Sa’ad bin ‘Ubadah r.a.asked Rasulullah SAW,” O’ Rasulullah PBUH, what form of sadqah is best?” He replied, “The gift of water.”

Health Facilities

Minimum health facilities have become an obstacle for our fellow Indonesian in the east, living their lives under the fine line of poverty. They are without decent sanitation facilities and has no access to clean water.

Our offer of health program consists of malnutrition recovery, bengkel gizi, health services and the development of health facilities such as clinics.

Lend a helping hand, generate your best donation to evolve the quality of public health facilities in Papua, East Nusa Tenggara, West Nusa Tenggara, Sulawesi, and Sumatera.

INFRASTRUCTURE DEVELOPMENT

The lack of decent public facilities/ infrastructure in underdeveloped location is a continuation of heartaches for the people living in the area. This is because the lack of infrastructures such as bridges and or public facilities will make it difficult for residents to better their economic situation in contrast with urban areas
