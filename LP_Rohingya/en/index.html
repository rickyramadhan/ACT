About Rohingya

Rohingya is a native ethnic Muslim group which resides in Arakan since XVI CE. The area is currently known as part of Rakhine State, Western Myanmar, immediately bordering with Bangladesh. Etymologically, Rohingya came from the word Rohai or Roshangee which means the people of Rohang or Roshang (later called Arakan).

Since before the independence of Myanmar, the Rohingya has been the victims of mass annihilation attempts. In 2012, Rohingya Elimination Group initiated by extremist group 969 emerged. Conflict between two parties broke, killing 200 people and over 140,000 Rohingyans are forced to live in inhumane concentration camps. According to study by International State Crime Initiative (ISCI) from Queen Mary University of London, the Rohingya has entered the last stages of genocide which include mass annihilation and symbolic enactment. United Nations also declared the Rohingya as the most tormented ethnic group on earth.

At the moment, Rohingya Muslims in Rakhine live in fear and isolation. Since 2013, thousands of people have been fleeing to countries such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand by boats. Men, women, and children are stranded on the sea without having any idea where to go and whether the people at faraway land would accept them. One of the Rohingya refugee camps in Indonesia was built by ACT Foundation in Blang Adoe, North Aceh.

Chronology

History of genocide on Rohingya Muslims since pre-independence of Myanmar until present days.





1942: Mass slaughter of Pro-British Rohingya Muslims

Happened during Japan occupation before Myanmar independence. Over 100,000 Rohingya Muslims were killed and thousands of villages destroyed.

1948: Independence of Myanmar from Great Britain

1978: King Dragon Operation

Aimed to intimidate the Rohingyas and force them out of Arakan. Over 200,000 people fled to Bangladesh.

1982: Myanmar denied citizenship of Rohingya

Rohingya wasnâ€™t included to 135 official ethnic groups of Myanmar.

1990s: Repatriation

200,000 Rohingyas who fled to Bangladesh were repatriated.

1994: Myanmar stopped issuance of birth certificate for Rohingya children

2001: Destroying of mosques and schools

28 mosques and Islamic schools in Maungdaw were destroyed.

2012: Rohingya Elimination Group emerged

Initiated by extremist group 969. Aimed to annihilate the Rohingya from Arakan. Over 140,000 people were herded to concentration camps, 200 people died.

2013: Great exodus

Thousands of Rohingyas fled the country by boats to Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand.

2015: Boat crisis in Andaman Sea

Thousands of people were stranded on the sea, many of them died before reaching land. UNHCR estimated over 150,000 people fled Myanmar-Bangladesh border since January 2012.

2016: Muslim Rohingya purge

Per October, 150 people were killed and 3 villages burned down.

*Source: International State Crime Initiative (ISCI) report, 2015 dan A History of Arakan oleh Mohammed Yunus, 1994
